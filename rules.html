      Insurance & Waiver

      All teams must carry team insurance purchased through Small Town Select, or add Small Town Select as additionally insured on their existing team insurance. All coaches complete the season Team Registration before registering and paying for any tournaments. Tournaments are first-pay, first-play.

      Baseball Rules 2026

      Tournaments run as Rec All-Star, Minors, Triple-A, or Open, grouping teams of the same age in pool play.

      Eligibility & Insurance
      • Birth certificates required upon request.
      • Insurance is mandatory: minimum $100,000 excess accident/medical and $2,000,000 general liability, with Small Town Select listed as additionally insured.
      • Tournament age is determined as of May 1 of the current year; grade-based exceptions are available with a birth certificate and proof of current grade.
      • A player may "play up" in age, but under no circumstance may a player "play down" in age.
      Rosters
      • Players may participate with only one team per event. Violators face removal from all teams and manager ejection.
      • Use full legal names (first & last) as on the birth certificate, plus date of birth (mm/dd/yyyy). No nicknames, shortened names, or initials.
      • Rosters are frozen prior to each event. An ineligible rostered player results in removal and head-coach ejection.
      Bats & Equipment

      Maximum 34" length and 2¾" barrel across all divisions. Aluminum/composite bats must bear "BPF 1.15," "BBCOR," or "USA" stamping for 14U and under; wood, composite, and bamboo bats are also permitted.

      • 14U maximum −5 drop; 13U maximum −8 drop.
      • Prohibited: "T-Ball Only," "Coach Pitch" labeled, "Not for Use with Real Baseballs," and altered bats.
      • Metal cleats permitted in 13U–14U only, and never on portable mounds.
      Lineup, Substitutions & DH/EH

      Minimum 8 players to start. Lineup options: straight 9 (+1 sub), 9 with DH (+1 sub), 10 with DH and EH (+1 sub), or bat the entire roster.

      • NFHS substitution rules apply; starters may re-enter once in their original batting order.
      • DH available 9U–14U. Extra Hitter (EH) available 9–14, declared before the game; a team that starts with an EH must keep a 10-player lineup all game.
      • Defensive timeouts: 2 per half-inning (pitching change required on the 2nd). Offensive timeouts: 1 per half-inning.
      • Slash bunts not allowed 9U–13U (out + head-coach ejection); 14U only on a 60/90 field.
      • Courtesy runners allowed for pitcher and catcher (not after a pitch is made to the next batter).
      Pitching

      No mandatory pitch limits; recommendations only. Two mound visits per inning max — a second visit to the same pitcher in the same inning requires a pitching change. Intentional walks may be awarded without pitches. Balks are an automatic dead ball.

      • 9U–10U: 6 outs or less to pitch the next day; 18 outs max per tournament.
      • 11U–14U: 6 outs or less to pitch the next day; 21 outs max per tournament.

      Tiebreakers & Seeding

      Bracket seeding order: 1) record, 2) head-to-head (two teams only), 3) runs allowed, 4) run differential (10 max per game), 5) runs scored, 6) automated coin flip. Highest seed is home in bracket games.

      Coach Pitch (7U–8U), Kid Pitch (8U) & 6U

      7U–8U Coach Pitch: adult pitches overhand from the rubber to their own team; max 6 pitches / 3 swinging strikes; 10 defenders (4 outfielders held until the ball is hit); 7-run inning cap; no leadoffs/steals/bunting; one intentional walk per game on request.

      8U Kid Pitch: no leadoffs or stealing home; walks are a dead ball (batter to 1st); no dropped-3rd-strike advance; no balks or bunts; 7-run inning cap.

      6U Modified Coach Pitch: 5 coach pitches then one tee attempt; 10-foot pitching circle; 10 defenders (4 outfielders); 7-run cap except the final inning.

      Conduct & Ejections
      • 1st ejection: leave the park immediately, ineligible for the next game. 2nd: ineligible for the next two games and barred from the park for the rest of the tournament.
      • 3rd cumulative team ejection disqualifies the entire team and may carry a permanent STS suspension.
      • Malicious contact is a judgment call: runner out, ejection, and one-game suspension. Razzing/heckling, profanity, and pulling a team off the field (auto-forfeit) are prohibited.
      • Security calls, post-game misconduct, or property damage may result in disqualification with no refund and possible legal action.
      Rainout / Refund Policy

      1–2 day tournaments: 0 games played = full refund · 1 game played = 50% refund · 2 games played = no refund. A game counts as "played" once it starts. No individual gate refunds.

      Softball Rules NCS

      Format: 2 pool games into a double-elimination bracket. PAP $40 (60-minute games) or $45 (75-minute games).

      Pool Play
      • Two 60-minute games, finishing the inning; pool games may end in a tie.
      • Maximum 6 runs per half-inning. Run rules: 10 after 3, 8 after 4, 7 after 5.
      • Bat the full lineup; free defensive substitutions. Courtesy runners must be substitutes (or the last batted-out player if none).
      Bracket Play (Double Elimination)
      • 75-minute games, finishing the inning — must have a winner.
      • International tiebreaker: last batted-out runner to 2nd, no outs, no count.
      • No per-inning run rule (10U–14U). Run rules: 12 after 3, 10 after 4, 8 after 5.
      • Batting options: 9, 11 with 2 EH, 10 with 1 EH and DP/Flex, or the entire roster. Free defensive substitutions.
      Rainout / Refund Policy

      No games = 100% refund · one game = 50% refund · two or more = no refund (awards by pool results).
